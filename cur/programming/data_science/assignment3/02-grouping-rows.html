So far we have learned about several helpful querying methods, but there is a useful ability these blocks do not yet provide us. This is the ability to group rows based on their values in a given row, which can help us learn about the contents of a dataset. In the taxis example from Assignments 1 and 2, this may have involved grouping the rides based on pickup_borough to see the distribution of where rides originated.

In this assginment, we will introduce the GROUP and PIVOT blocks as 2 different ways to groups rows and learn about a dataset. This will be particularly important in the next assignment when we talk about visualizing data. Being able to categorize data based on a given column's information will allow us to visualize different characteristics of the data in ways that non-programmers (the majority of people who interact with data are people unfamiliar with programming) will understand.
