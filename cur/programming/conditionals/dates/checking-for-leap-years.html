A year in our calendar is a leap year—it has 366 days—if it is either divisible, with no remainder, by 400 or divisible by 4 but not by 100. Thus 2000 was a leap year (divisible by 400, 100, and 4), 1900 was not (divisible by 100 and 4), 1996 was (divisible by 4), and 1999 (not divisible by anything!) wasn't.
