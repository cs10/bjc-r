After a while, you'll notice that your randomly moving sprite will move off the screen. Add to your script so that the sprite stays within 100 pixels of the center of the screen (that is, in a 200 by 200 square).

You can do using an if block, and will find the the , , and reporter blocks useful.

As a first step, make sure your character doesn't go below the y = -100 line—that is, doesn't have a y position less than -100. If the character is below the y = -100 line, they should not go any lower. That is, don't allow any directions that are between 90 and 180 degrees. (If you are using negative numbers for direction, you'll need to check for additional values!)

If your find that your sprite is too low and will go lower on the current move, you can either:

  • simply not move this step, and have the sprite choose a new, random direction on the next step.
  • use a turn statement to fix the direction, by flipping it so it points upward.

Test your solution long enough to ensure that your sprite won't go below the y = -100 line—or, won't continue once a move takes it below. One trick that might speed up testing: drag your sprite below the y=-100 line, and see if it only moves upwards. To additionally help your testing, consider drawing a red 200 pixel by 200 pixel box on the screen, so you can see if your sprite crosses it.

You'll need to handle the other three directions as well:

  • If the sprite is above the y = 100 line, they should not go any higher.
  • If the sprite is to the left of the x = -100 line, they should not go any farther left.
  • If the sprite is to the right of the x = 100 line, they should not go any farther right.
