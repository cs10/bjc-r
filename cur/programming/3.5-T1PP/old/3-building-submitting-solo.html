Building and Submitting Your Individual Program

  1. Choose a new project to develop by yourself or design a new feature for the project that you developed with your partner.
  2. Work by yourself to develop your program. Be sure to use both:
      HELP!!! --MF
    • abstraction—showing the structure of the method
    • an algorithm—a set of steps that the computer uses to solve a problem
  3. When you are finished, put your code in a PDF, and send it to your teacher.
  4. Create a video of your program running. It should be less than 1 minute and include a description of the program either as text or a voice recording. Send your video to your teacher.
  5. Write out your own personal reflections on the individual and team programming projects. Do each of the following:
    • Describe the purpose of your individual program and state that you used Snap! to write it. (100 words or less)
    • Describe how you and your partner shared the work on the pair part of this project. (100 words or less)
    • Describe the most significant piece of feedback that you provided to your partner. (50 words or less)
    • Describe the most significant piece of feedback that your partner provided to you. (50 words or less)
