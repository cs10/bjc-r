  3. Create a video (two options below) of your program running. It should be less than 1 minute and include a description of the program either as text or a voice recording. Send the video to your teacher.
    • You can create your video by using a screen-recording program like QuickTime Player (on a Mac) or by downloading a program that your teacher recommends (on a PC).
    • Or you can use a phone, tablet, or video camera to record your program in action.
  4. Write out your reflections on the pair programming project. As a team, do each of the following:
    • Describe the purpose of your program and state that you used Snap! to write it. (100 words or less)
    • Describe how your project uses abstraction: (100 words or less)
      • Include an image of part of your program that uses abstraction.
      • Describe how this part of your program uses abstraction.
    • Describe how your project uses a complex algorithm: (100 words or less)
      • Include an image of the most complicated algorithm in your program.
      • Describe what that algorithm does.
    • Describe how you created your program step-by-step. What did you do first? Next? What challenges did you run into and how did you solve them? (100 words or less)
