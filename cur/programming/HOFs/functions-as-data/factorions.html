A number is called a factorion if it is equal to the sum of the factorials of its digits. For example, 145 is a factorion since 145 = 1! + 4! + 5! (Remember that n! is the fancy, mathematical way of denoting the factorial of n, which is the product of all of the numbers from 1 to the number n.) Another, relatively simpler example of a factorion is 2, since 2 is equal to 2! (Weï¿½re not exclaiming there.)
