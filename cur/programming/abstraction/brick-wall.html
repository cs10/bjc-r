Abstraction

Consider the above picture. A person sees a cartoon cat and a brick wall. The computer sees 0s and 1s. It would be very difficult (and time-consuming!) for a person to write out all the 0s and 1s needed to tell the computer how to draw the brick wall. A programming language, such as Snap!, makes it easier to tell the computer what to do. It masks away all the details of the 0s and 1s through a process called abstraction.

While Snap! has lots of blocks for drawing and moving, it doesn't have anything about bricks. It wouldn't make sense to have one in general because most programs don't involve bricks. But ones that do could really use a block. is English and moves us even further away from having to talk like a computer. The process of moving from what the programming language gives you to a natural language (one that humans speak to communicate--in this case, English) is critical to a good abstraction.

Appendix: The Mathematics of Drawing the Brick Wall

This is not intended to be a math class, but it is worth looking at the math for this problem. It might look like a short brick should be half the length of a long brick, but it's not! Let's see why.

Let R be the total length of a row of bricks, L be the length of a Long brick, S be the length of a Short brick, and M be the amount of Mortar (represented in our pictures as space) between bricks. We get two equations:

Row A equation: R = 6L + 5M
Row B equation: R = 5L + 6M + 2S

Row A minus Row B: 0 = L - M - 2S or L = M + 2S

Because we started two equations and more than two unknowns, we actually have an infinite number of possible ways to solve these equations! Which means that we have an infinite number of ways to draw a brick wall. However, we are constrained by the size of our display, which is 480x360. You can choose whatever L, M, and S values you like as long as your brick wall fits nicely into the display and L = M + 2S.

In the pictures of bricks, L = 40, S = 12, and M = 16, but any combination of L, M, and S can work as long as L = M + 2S.
