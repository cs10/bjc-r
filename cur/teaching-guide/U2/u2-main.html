Week 5 (Programming Focus: Predicates, Script Variables, and Program Structure)

In preparation for Week 5:
  • The first time you teach this unit, do the first few two or three problems on each page of programming labs 1 and 2 yourself, to get a sense of the time it might take and to remind you of the contents.
  • One focus of this entire unit is analyzing the task, breaking it into simpler chunks, and building those chunks independently. A second focus is debugging. One useful way to help when students get stuck is for you not to "know" the answer. You can be a bit "slow" in understanding their program and "naive" about solving the problem. Ask questions like "What is it supposed to do?" "What is it doing?" "What part of your result don't you like?" "What part of your program could possibly be doing that?" "Explain your script. What is each block doing?" "Can you make something that does almost what you want?" "Who got problem x? Can you explain how you did it?"
  • Some of the projects will have subtleties, and/or will be slightly more complex than in unit 1.
During Week 5, students will work on Labs 1-2 of Unit 2.
  • Programming Lab 1: Conditional Blocks (student pages) (teacher support) ~ five student pages in roughly three (55 minute) periods
    • Discuss: SOMETHING
  • Programming Lab 2: Script Variables (student pages) (teacher support) ~ three student pages in roughly two (55 minute) periods
    • Share: OTHER THINGS

Week 7 (Social Implications Focus): Privacy BtB3

In preparation for Week 7:
  • There are many options for students to access Blown to Bits. They can read it as an online PDF or you may choose to purchase hard copies of the book. You may also photocopy the book for students so that they can make annotations as they read.
  • Other suggestions
Students discuss and write about issues of Community and Online Interactions and Privacy.
  • Social Implications Lab 7: Privacy (student pages) (teacher support) ~ 120 minutes
    • Discuss: Why do we need privacy? What is the case against privacy?
    • Discuss: What are the different ways our privacy is affected by computers?
    • Read: Blown to Bits: "Footprints and Fingerprints"
    • Discuss: What are some innovations (good and bad) that we give information to?
    • Read: "Addicted to Apps"
  • Social Implications Lab 8: Community and Online Interactions (student pages) (teacher support) ~ 120 minutes
    • Discuss: What do you know about how Twitter works? What questions do you have about how it works?
    • Discuss: What are some different ways we communicate?
    • Write: Choose a good innovation and say why ok to give up privacy for it. And choose an innovation and say why we should be cautious of how much privacy we give up for it.
    • Respond: Read each others' writing and write a response.
