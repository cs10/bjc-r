Openers

  • Make a Prediction
    Students predict the result of a piece of code. Students reason through the logic of code. Teachers might use these to introduce a new concept or address a common misconception. Students reason through the code without the use of a computer. The code may include elements they have not yet used or complicated logic to sort through.
  • Debugging
    Students are presented with code containing an error and must debug it. Students gain experience with debugging and must make sense of code. Teachers might use these to point to a common pitfall or teach a debugging strategy. Students reason through code without the use of a computer. The code includes bugs for students to resolve.
  • Computing in the News
    Students are presented with and discuss a current news story about computing. Students gain a greater sense of the relevance of computing in society, are exposed to complex social issues, and learn to read news stories with an informed and critial eye. (More about this below.)
  • Snap Judgment
    Students write down their view in response to a prompt about a Social Implications topic. This is not meant to be polished writing, or even take a view that students firmly believe. The purpose is to engage students and support written communication skills. Throughout the year, students should develop their ability to state a position and provide evidence to support it.

Computing in the News Sources

Find appropriate news articles to share with the class. Choose a mix of news stories, some which show the promise of technology and others which expose negative aspects.

  • ACM TechNews Archives (updated 3 times a week)
    • If you subscribe to this newsletter, it will deliver a digest to your inbox three times a week. There are a range of topics, and going through this email with students is one way to conduct a Computing in the News opener. This is a good way to touch on a wide variety of computing ideas and areas of interest for students.
  • Technology Section of the New York Times
    • Going to a news site and skimming through an interesting article together is another way to conduct a Computing in the News opener. Go through the article, highlighting some major points, then ask students to respond to some of the ideas.
    • As you present news stories, ask students to respond to them:
    • What are some of the implications of the subject of the article?
    • Do you agree with the views presented?
    • What ideas would you be interested in exploring further?
