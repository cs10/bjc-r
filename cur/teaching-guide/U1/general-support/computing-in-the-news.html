Computing in the News

Find appropriate news articles to share with the class. Choose a mix of news stories—some which show the promise of technology and others which expose negative aspects.

  • If you subscribe to a newsletter (such as ACM TechNews), you will regularly receive a digest in your inbox. Going through this email with students is one way to conduct a Computing in the News opener. This is a good way to touch on a wide variety of computing ideas and areas of interest for students.
  • Going to a news site (such as the Technology Section of the New York Times) and skimming through for an interesting article together is another way to conduct a Computing in the News opener. Go through the article, highlighting some major points, then ask students to respond to some of the ideas.

As you present news stories, ask students:

  • What are some of the implications of the subject of the article?
  • Do you agree with the views presented?
  • What ideas would you be interested in exploring further?
